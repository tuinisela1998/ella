Falefa is a large village on the north east coast of Upolu island in Samoa. Falefa means the "four houses", indicative of the four sub-villages which make up the village itself; Sanone, Gagaemalae, Saleapaga, Sagapolu. Each of these sub villages comprise families, the heads of which, represent their extended families and their respective area at Falefa's Village Council, headed by the two senior tulafale-alii of the village, Moeono & Iuli. Falefa is also known for its beauty by the waterfalls there, the falls are in the distance and flow out to the sea in the other direction.
